Category 1

Pharmacovigilance & regulations

Pharmacovigilance is the science and activities relating to the detection, assessment, understanding and prevention of adverse effects of medicines. It is mandatory for all Marketing Authorisation Holders (MAHs) under Regulation (EU) No 726/2004 and Directive 2001/83/CE, as amended by Regulation (EU) No 1235/2010. The goal is to protect public health through continuous monitoring of the benefit-risk profile of authorised medicines.

GVP (Good Pharmacovigilance Practices) are guidelines developed by the EMA to standardise pharmacovigilance practices across the European Union. They comprise 16 main modules covering all aspects of the pharmacovigilance system, from adverse reaction reporting to communication with authorities. MAC ensures full GVP compliance in all its activities.

An ICSR (Individual Case Safety Report) is a report relating to a single case of a suspected adverse reaction to a medicine. Reporting timelines vary: 15 calendar days for serious unexpected cases (SUSARs), 15 days for SAEs in clinical trials, and 90 days for non-serious cases. MAC manages the entire process, ensuring compliance with all regulatory timelines.

Category 2

Our services

The EU Qualified Person for Pharmacovigilance (EU-QPPV) is responsible for implementing and maintaining the pharmacovigilance system on behalf of the MAH. The role is mandatory for all EU MAH holders, and the person must reside within the EU. MAC provides an outsourced EU-QPPV service for companies of any size that need this function without employing a dedicated full-time person.

The PSUR (Periodic Safety Update Report) is the European format for periodic safety reports. The PBRER (Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Report) is the internationally harmonised ICH E2C(R2) format that has substantially replaced the PSUR at a global level. In the EU the term PSUR is still used but the structure follows the PBRER format. MAC drafts both formats, ensuring compliance with EMA and ICH requirements.
